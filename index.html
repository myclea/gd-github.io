Toolkit Introduction

The following analysis is generated by the Water-BOOST toolkit, designed to support and scale water innovation through a two-stage process:

Stage 1 – Building the Minimal Viable System (MVS): This stage assesses whether all key stakeholder groups (such as utilities, policy-makers, innovators, and investors) and the enablers that connect them are present in a given ecosystem. These enablers include both individual mechanisms (between specific actors) and multi-stakeholder ones (supporting system-wide collaboration). Together, these elements form the foundational conditions for a functional water innovation ecosystem — or what we refer to as the Minimal Viable System (MVS).

Stage 2 – Tailored Technology Guidance: Once the MVS is established, the user is guided through a series of questions to determine the most suitable technology solutions, based on the specific characteristics, challenges, and priorities of each city.
